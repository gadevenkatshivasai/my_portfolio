INTERNET OF THINGS (IOT) AND CYBER-PHYSICAL SYSTEMS

The Internet of Things (IoT) and Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS) represent groundbreaking technological paradigms that converge to create a transformative impact on various domains. IoT involves the seamless interconnection of an extensive array of devices, sensors, and systems through the internet, fostering data exchange and automation. In parallel, CPS integrates computational algorithms with physical processes, orchestrating a symbiotic relationship between the digital and physical realms. This abstract provides an overview of the key principles, applications, and challenges associated with the integration of IoT and CPS. The interplay between IoT and CPS unlocks unprecedented opportunities for enhanced monitoring, real-time data analysis, and intelligent decision-making. This integration facilitates the development of adaptive systems capable of responding dynamically to the evolving environment. Applications span diverse sectors, including healthcare, manufacturing, transportation, and smart cities, where the combined capabilities of IoT and CPS contribute to increased efficiency, resource optimization, and improved user experiences. However, this transformative synergy presents challenges that warrant careful consideration. Security concerns, interoperability issues, and the need for standardized frameworks emerge as critical aspects that demand attention. This abstract explores both the promises and challenges of IoT and CPS integration, highlighting the potential societal and economic impacts of this technological convergence. As the digital and physical worlds continue to intertwine, understanding and addressing these challenges will be crucial in harnessing the full potential of IoT and CPS for a smarter, more connected future.
